What's Actually Happening Out There
The Stack Gets Filtered in 6 Seconds

Before a human reads your resume, software has already eliminated 75% of the pile. The candidates who survive aren't necessarily the most qualified — they're the ones who made themselves findable, credible, and undeniable across every touchpoint.

Google Is Part of the Interview

The first thing anyone does after seeing your resume is Google your name. What comes up? Your LinkedIn (which looks like everyone else's). Maybe an old photo. A career website built to tell your story changes what they find — and how they feel when they find it.

Credentials Alone Are Table Stakes

Everyone in your candidate pool has a degree and a list of jobs. The question isn't what you've done — it's how compellingly you can make a hiring manager care about it. That's a writing problem. And most people are terrible at writing about themselves.

The Narrative Gap Is Real and Exploitable

You have a story. You just haven't told it properly. There's almost always a through-line in a career that, once identified and articulated, makes someone go from "qualified applicant" to "the obvious choice." We find that thread. We pull it.

FAQ

THE REAL QUESTIONS

Stuff people actually want to know before they hand us a thousand dollars and their entire career narrative.

Who writes the story?

A human being with 25+ years of experience in journalism, copywriting, and editorial leadership. The process is human-driven and AI-assisted — we use AI tools to accelerate research and surface patterns in your career history, but every word of your narrative is shaped, refined, and owned by a person who understands storytelling. You'll never get a paste-and-publish AI output.

What if I hate the first draft?

One round of full revisions is included. In practice, most clients want small adjustments — a section rewritten, a stat added, a tone shift. We've never had to start over entirely, but if it came to that, we'd handle it.

How long does it take?

Story delivery within 7 business days of your intake call. Site delivery within 7 business days of story approval. You could be live in two weeks. Rush options available if you have an interview coming up.

What if I'm not actively job hunting?

Smart. The best time to build this is before you need it. Recruiters don't only come calling when you're desperate. Your site is also useful for speaking opportunities, client pitches, press inquiries, and board seats.

Do I own the site?

Yes, completely. If you cancel hosting, we give you the full HTML file and domain transfer. It's yours. We're not a platform that holds your career hostage.

Is this only for senior people?

The sweet spot is mid-to-senior professionals — 8+ years of experience, real accomplishments to build a story around. That said, if you have a compelling story, we can tell it regardless of where you are in your career.
